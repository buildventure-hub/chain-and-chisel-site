FAQ

What kind of wood do you carve?

Most carvings are created from locally sourced logs such as pine, cottonwood, or other suitable species depending on availability. If you have a tree being removed, I can often carve directly from your stump or salvaged log.

Do you take custom commissions?

Yes. Each piece is carved to order based on your space, idea, and budget. You’re welcome to reach out through the Contact page to submit a request, leave a review, or simply share an idea.

How much does a carving cost?

Pricing depends on size, wood type, level of detail, and finish. Smaller yard sculptures are more accessible, while larger custom pieces are quoted individually. Share your idea and I’ll provide a clear estimate.

How long does a carving take?

Smaller pieces may take a few hours to a day. Larger commissions can take multiple sessions. Weather, wood condition, and drying time can affect scheduling.

Can you carve from my existing tree stump?

Often, yes. The stump’s size, wood type, and condition determine whether it’s suitable. If you’re unsure, send a photo and I’ll let you know.

Are carvings sealed for outdoor use?

Yes. Outdoor pieces are burned, treated, and sealed to help withstand the elements. Periodic maintenance will extend their life and appearance.

Do you deliver?

Local delivery may be available depending on size and distance. Larger sculptures may require special transport arrangements.

Can you recreate something I saw online?

I can create something inspired by an idea, but each carving is original. The goal is to make your piece unique rather than duplicate someone else’s exact design.

How do I get started?

Visit the Contact page and share your idea, approximate size, location (for delivery or stump carving), and timeline. I’ll follow up with next steps and a quote.
